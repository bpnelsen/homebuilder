# 10-Q Quarterly Report Summaries — US Homebuilders
*Compiled by Seeker Agent — March 17, 2026*

---

# Lennar Corporation (LEN)
**Q3 FY2025 (ended Aug 31, 2025) — Filed Oct 3, 2025**

Revenue: $8.25B for Q3, $8.81B for 9 months. Net Income (9 months): $1.81B.

Lennar reported solid Q3 results with total revenues of $8.25B for the quarter. Home deliveries remained robust across all segments. Financial services segment continues to contribute meaningful pre-tax earnings with mortgage capture rate above 80%. The company maintains a strong balance sheet with net homebuilding debt-to-capital below 10%. Continued focus on entry-level and move-up buyers with land-light strategy using options for 82% of lots.

---

# D.R. Horton (DHI)
**Q1 FY2026 (ended Dec 31, 2025) — Filed Jan 22, 2026**

Net Income: $601M for the quarter, down from prior year.

Revenue declined year-over-year as the company navigated challenging rate environment. Net orders showed modest improvement quarter-over-quarter. Strong cash flow from operations maintained. Rental segment continues growing with additional communities in development. Conservative balance sheet with strong liquidity. Focus on affordable, entry-level homes with spec-built model for quick inventory turns.

---

# KB Home (KBH)
**Q3 FY2025 (ended Aug 31, 2025) — Filed Oct 9, 2025**

Revenue: $1.62B for Q3. Net Income: $109.8M (Q3), $327.3M (9 months).

Revenue of $1.62B reflects continued delivery volumes despite rate headwinds. Net income declined from prior year as company increased buyer incentives. Backlog conversion remains steady. Built-to-order model differentiates from spec competitors. Energy efficiency commitment as key selling point. Sun Belt markets provide long-term demographic tailwinds.

# Toll Brothers (TOL)
**Q1 FY2026 (ended Jan 31, 2026) — Filed Feb 27, 2026**

Net Income: $210.9M for the quarter.

Luxury segment showing resilience despite rate environment. Higher-end buyers are less rate-sensitive. Signed contracts mixed across geographies. Diversification into rental and student housing continues. Solid balance sheet with adequate liquidity. Geographic diversification across Northeast, California, Florida, and Sun Belt.

# PulteGroup (PHM)
**Q3 FY2025 (ended Sep 30, 2025) — Filed Oct 21, 2025**

Revenue: $4.40B for Q3. Net Income: $585.8M (Q3), $1.72B (9 months).

Revenue of $4.40B reflects steady deliveries. Del Webb active adult segment continues to outperform, benefiting from Boomer demographics. Pulte Mortgage maintains strong capture rates. Strong balance sheet with conservative leverage. Three-brand segmentation provides flexibility across buyer demographics.

---

# NVR Inc (NVR)
**Q3 FY2025 (ended Sep 30, 2025) — Filed Nov 5, 2025**

Net Income: $342.7M (Q3), $976.0M (9 months).

Asset-light model continues to provide downside protection. Land option model minimizes balance sheet risk. Aggressive share repurchases continue. Among the strongest balance sheets in the industry. No dividends — all capital returned via buybacks. Mid-Atlantic market leadership provides competitive advantages.

---

# Tri Pointe Homes (TPH)
**Q3 FY2025 (ended Sep 30, 2025) — Filed Oct 23, 2025**

Revenue: $836.9M for Q3. Net Income: $56.1M (Q3), $180.8M (9 months).

Western US markets remain challenging. California and Colorado particularly impacted by affordability constraints. Net income down significantly from prior year. Design differentiation and lifestyle positioning. Geographic expansion into Carolinas provides diversification.

---

# Cavco Industries (CVCO)
**Q3 FY2026 (ended Dec 27, 2025) — Filed Feb 2, 2026**

Net Income: $44.1M for Q3.

Continues to outperform site-built builders with factory-built model advantages. Cost and speed advantages over traditional construction. Financial services segment contributes meaningful revenue. Healthy balance sheet with modest leverage. Factory-built housing addresses affordable housing crisis.

---

# LGI Homes (LGIH)
**Q3 FY2025 (ended Sep 30, 2025) — Filed Nov 4, 2025**

Net Income: $19.7M (Q3), $55.2M (9 months).

Entry-level segment continues to face headwinds. First-time buyers most sensitive to mortgage rate increases. Higher incentives and rate buydowns required. Debt-to-capital elevated vs peers due to land-heavy strategy. When rates decline, LGI benefits disproportionately from pent-up first-time buyer demand.
