# 10-K Annual Report Summaries — US Homebuilders
*Compiled by Johnny Agent — March 17, 2026*
*Source: SEC Edgar filings*

# Lennar Corporation (LEN)
*Fiscal Year Ended November 30, 2025 (Filed 2026-01-28)*

**Revenue:** $33.78B — increased 29% year-over-year from $26.26B. **Net Income (pre-tax):** $2.81B — declined 46% YoY from $5.18B.

**Company Overview:** Lennar is one of the largest homebuilders in the US, operating in 26 states across 75 active markets. The company operates through Homebuilding, Financial Services, and Multifamily segments.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Lennar delivered 83,978 homes in FY2025, up 9% from 76,951 in FY2024. Revenue grew 29% driven by strong closing volumes and stable pricing. However, net earnings before taxes declined significantly, reflecting margin compression from higher incentives and mortgage rate buydowns used to drive sales.

**Balance Sheet:** Homebuilding debt-to-total capital improved to 15.7%, with net homebuilding debt-to-capital at just 2.8% — essentially debt-free on a net basis. Stockholders' equity of $22.0B provides a strong foundation. Backlog of 13,936 homes valued at $5.24B entering FY2026.

**Strategy:** Lennar continues focusing on affordable, entry-level and move-up homes. Financial services segment contributed $612M in pre-tax earnings with an 84% mortgage capture rate. The company maintains a disciplined land strategy with 82% optioned lots.

# D.R. Horton, Inc. (DHI)
*Fiscal Year Ended September 30, 2025 (Filed 2025-11-19)*

**Revenue:** $34.3B — declined 7% year-over-year from $36.8B. **Net Income:** $3.62B — declined 25% YoY from $4.81B.

**Company Overview:** D.R. Horton is the largest homebuilder in the United States by volume, operating through multiple brands including D.R. Horton (move-up), Express Homes (entry-level), Freedom Homes, and Emerald Homes (luxury). The company also has rental and financial services segments.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Consolidated revenues decreased 7% to $34.3 billion from $36.8 billion in fiscal 2024. Despite the revenue decline, DHI maintained strong profitability with consolidated pre-tax income of $3.6B. Cash flow from operations remained robust at $3.4 billion, up from $2.2 billion in the prior year.

**Balance Sheet:** DHI maintains a conservative balance sheet with strong liquidity. The company's scale advantages in purchasing, overhead leverage, and land pipeline provide competitive cost advantages. Homebuilding return on equity remains among the highest in the industry.

**Strategy:** DHI focuses on affordable, entry-level homes — the segment with the strongest demographic demand. The company uses a spec-built model with quick inventory turns. Geographic diversification across 119 markets provides resilience.

# KB Home (KBH)
*Fiscal Year Ended November 30, 2025 (Filed 2026-01-23)*

**Revenue:** $6.24B — declined 10% year-over-year from $6.93B. **Net Income:** $428.8M — declined 35% YoY from $655.0M.

**Company Overview:** KB Home operates a unique built-to-order model in the homebuilding industry, allowing buyers to personalize their homes. The company primarily serves first-time and move-up buyers in the Western and Southeastern United States.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Total revenues declined 10% driven by reduced delivery volumes in a challenging mortgage rate environment. Net income fell 35% as the company navigated margin pressure from buyer incentives and higher mortgage rate buydowns.

**Balance Sheet:** Debt-to-capital ratio of 30.3% is moderate for the industry. The company maintains adequate liquidity to fund operations and land acquisition. Cash position declined from prior year as the company invested in lot positions for future growth.

**Strategy:** KBH's built-to-order approach differentiates it from spec-heavy competitors, though it requires longer cycle times. The company focuses on energy-efficient homes and sustainability features. Geographic concentration in high-growth Sun Belt markets (Arizona, California, Nevada, Texas, Florida, Colorado, the Carolinas) positions it well for demographic tailwinds.

# Toll Brothers, Inc. (TOL)
*Fiscal Year Ended October 31, 2025 (Filed 2025-12-19)*

**Revenue:** $10.97B — relatively flat year-over-year from $10.85B. **Net Income:** $1.35B — declined 14% YoY from $1.57B.

**Company Overview:** Toll Brothers is the nation's leading builder of luxury homes, operating in over 50 markets across 24 states. The company also has growing segments in rental, student housing, and urban development.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Revenue held relatively steady while net income declined 14%, reflecting margin compression from higher incentives and mortgage rate buydowns used to support luxury buyers. Signed contracts showed mixed results across markets.

**Balance Sheet:** Toll Brothers maintains a solid balance sheet with sufficient liquidity. The luxury segment typically generates higher margins per home but with longer cycle times and more exposed to discretionary buyer sentiment.

**Strategy:** Toll Brothers differentiates through luxury positioning, architectural design, and customization. The company has diversified beyond traditional for-sale homes into rental communities (including student housing), urban condo development, and golf course communities. The luxury buyer is less rate-sensitive but more impacted by stock market wealth effects and economic confidence.

# PulteGroup, Inc. (PHM)
*Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2025 (Filed 2026-02-04)*

**Revenue:** $17.31B — declined 4% year-over-year from $17.95B. **Net Income:** $2.22B — declined 28% YoY from $3.08B.

**Company Overview:** PulteGroup operates through three nationally recognized brands: Pulte Homes (move-up buyers), Del Webb (active adult/55+), and Centex (first-time buyers). The company also operates Pulte Mortgage for in-house financing.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Total revenues declined modestly at 4%, outperforming several peers. Net income fell 28% reflecting broader industry margin compression. The Del Webb active adult segment has been a standout performer, benefiting from aging demographics and Baby Boomer retirement trends.

**Balance Sheet:** PulteGroup maintains a strong balance sheet with conservative leverage and robust cash generation. The company's three-brand strategy provides flexibility to pivot across buyer segments based on market conditions.

**Strategy:** PulteGroup's brand segmentation is a key competitive advantage — Del Webb's 55+ focus provides recession-resistant demand from equity-rich retirees, while Pulte Homes and Centex serve the broader market. Pulte Mortgage's integration provides competitive capture rates and margin contribution.

# NVR, Inc. (NVR)
*Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2025 (Filed 2026-02-11)*

**Net Income:** $1.34B — declined 20% year-over-year from $1.68B.

**Company Overview:** NVR operates through Ryan Homes, NVHomes, and Heartland Homes, primarily in the Mid-Atlantic, Northeast, and Midwest regions. NVR's business model is uniquely asset-light — the company options land rather than purchasing it outright.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Net income declined 20% to $1.34B from $1.68B, reflecting the challenging housing market environment. NVR's asset-light model provides downside protection but also limits upside in strong markets. The company does not pay dividends, instead aggressively repurchasing shares.

**Balance Sheet:** NVR's balance sheet is among the strongest in the industry. The land option model minimizes balance sheet risk and capital requirements. This approach proved its value during the 2008 housing crisis when NVR was one of the few builders to remain profitable.

**Strategy:** NVR's differentiated land strategy is its defining characteristic — by controlling lots through options rather than ownership, the company can walk away from bad deals with minimal losses. Conservative approach with exceptional resilience. Strong focus on operational efficiency, construction quality, and market-leading positions in Mid-Atlantic markets. Share buybacks have reduced share count dramatically over time, amplifying per-share earnings growth.

# Tri Pointe Homes, Inc. (TPH)
*Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2025 (Filed 2026-02-26)*

**Revenue:** $3.40B — declined 23% year-over-year from $4.42B. **Net Income:** $241M — declined 47% YoY from $458M.

**Company Overview:** Tri Pointe Homes operates in 10 states with primary focus on the Western US, Texas, and Colorado. The company positions itself as a lifestyle brand emphasizing design and innovation.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Revenue declined significantly at 23%, among the steeper drops in the industry, reflecting geographic exposure to markets more impacted by affordability challenges. Net income was nearly halved.

**Balance Sheet:** The company maintains adequate liquidity but faces pressure from lower profitability. Book value per share has been declining alongside earnings.

**Strategy:** Tri Pointe focuses on design-forward homes with lifestyle appeal, targeting move-up buyers. The Western US and Texas focus provides exposure to high-growth markets but also creates concentration risk. The company has been working to improve operational efficiency and reduce cycle times. Geographic expansion into the Carolinas and Southeast provides diversification opportunities.

# Cavco Industries, Inc. (CVCO)
*Fiscal Year Ended March 29, 2025 (Filed 2025-05-23)*

**Net Income:** $171M — grew 8% year-over-year from $158M.

**Company Overview:** Cavco Industries is a leading manufacturer of factory-built housing, including manufactured homes, modular homes, and park model RVs. Unlike traditional site-built builders, Cavco constructs homes in factories and delivers them to sites.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Net income grew 8% to $171M from $158M — one of the few homebuilders to post earnings growth in the current environment. Cavco's manufactured housing model provides cost advantages and faster delivery times compared to site-built construction.

**Balance Sheet:** Cavco maintains a healthy balance sheet with modest leverage. The company's three segments — factory-built housing, financial services (mortgage lending and insurance), and retail — provide diversified revenue streams.

**Strategy:** Cavco's manufactured and modular housing approach addresses the affordable housing crisis with factory-controlled production efficiency. Homes are built faster, with less waste, and at lower cost than traditional construction. The manufactured housing segment benefits from growing acceptance and regulatory support for factory-built homes as a solution to housing affordability. Fiscal year ends in March.

# LGI Homes, Inc. (LGIH)
*Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2025 (Filed 2026-02-20)*

**Net Income:** $72.6M — declined 63% year-over-year from $196.1M.

**Company Overview:** LGI Homes focuses exclusively on entry-level and first-time homebuyers, positioning itself as the affordable homeownership specialist. The company operates in 36+ markets across 21 states.

**FY2025 Highlights:** Net income plummeted 63% — the steepest decline among major homebuilders. This dramatic drop reflects LGI's concentrated exposure to the entry-level segment, which has been hardest hit by mortgage rate increases and affordability constraints.

**Balance Sheet:** Debt-to-capital of 43.2% is elevated compared to peers, reflecting LGI's land-intensive strategy. The company owns more land and lots outright (rather than optioning), which amplifies both upside and downside. This higher leverage creates risk in a downturn but positions LGI for recovery.

**Strategy:** LGI's exclusive focus on entry-level buyers is both its strength and vulnerability. When affordability improves, LGI benefits disproportionately as first-time buyers return to the market. The company's direct-to-consumer marketing approach and no-commission sales model keep overhead low. Geographic expansion across Sun Belt and Southeast markets provides growth runway. The current cycle has been punishing for entry-level builders, but LGI's model should benefit significantly when mortgage rates decline and pent-up demand from Millennials is released.
